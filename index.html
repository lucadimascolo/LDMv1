About

Raised in the midst of floods of scooters, pasquinades, and the Colosseum, I was taught by Piero Angela to be fascinated with the wonders of the Cosmos. This brought me to move my first serious steps in science at the University of Rome "Tor Vergata". I then earned my Ph.D. at the Max-Planck-Institut für Astrophysik as an IMPRS Ph.D. student. I am now a post-doc at Università degli Studi di Trieste and Osservatorio Astronomico di Trieste. Here, I study the physical and thermodynamic properties of the hot, ionized gas residing in galaxy clusters, the "largest gravitationally-bound objects in our Universe".

Due to an unrivaled resolution, X-ray observations have long represented the traditional tool for studying the intracluster medium. However, recent years have witnessed tremendous progress in the measurement of the Sunyaev-Zeldovich (SZ) effect, the shadow cast on cosmic microwave background by the inverse Compton scattering of its photons off intracluster electrons. The Atacama Large Millimeter/Submillimeter Array (ALMA) has been a major player in making the SZ effect a powerful probe of the hot gas in our Universe. My current research is focused on modelling interferometric measurements of the SZ effect, thus to fully exploit ALMA unprecedented sensitivity and resolution.

In my copious free time away from the Fourier space, I shoot 35mm films with my loyal Canon AE-1, and obsess over folk music and sourdough bread.

Other

AtLAST
The Atacama Large Aperature Submillimeter/millimeter Telescope, a community-driven effort to realise a 50-meter class telescope in the Atacama desert. Currently no large (>12-meter) submm/mm single-dish facilities exist in the southern hemisphere. AtLAST would significantly advance submm/mm science, improving upon ALMA's mapping speed by over 4 orders of magnitude.

CMB-HD
A millimeter-wave survey over half the sky, spanning frequencies in the range of 30 to 350 GHz, and that is both an order of magnitude deeper and of higher-resolution than currently funded surveys.
